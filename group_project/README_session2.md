# Group Project — Session 2: Table 1 and group comparisons

This session is about turning curiosity into analysis. Pick a biological
question your group cares about, build a **Table 1** to describe the
participants, then use **gtsummary** and **ggpubr** to compare groups and
show results with p-values.

## Questions to answer as a group

- What biological question is your group most interested in?
- Which variable defines the **groups** you want to compare? (e.g., a
  treatment arm, a clinical category, a baseline status)
- Which demographic and baseline variables belong in your Table 1?
- Which outcome variables are most relevant to your question?
- For each outcome, what kind of comparison makes sense — two independent
  groups, paired measurements, or change over visits?
- Which `ggpubr` plot best shows that comparison? (boxplot, violin,
  paired-points, line plot…)
- What does a p-value mean in the context of your comparison? What does it
  *not* tell you?
- What surprised you in the results?
